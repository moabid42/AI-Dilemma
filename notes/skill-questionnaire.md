# AI Security Paper Questionnaire (for Custom Writing Skill)

## 1) Research framing
1. What exact working title do you want for this paper?
2. What is the main research question in one sentence?
3. What are 2-4 sub-questions you must answer?
4. Is the paper mostly conceptual, empirical, or design-oriented?
5. What is your target claim (the central thesis) you want the reader to accept?

## 2) Scope boundaries
6. Which AI systems are in scope (chatbot, RAG assistant, agentic workflow, multimodal system)?
7. Which are out of scope?
8. Do you focus on one case study organization or a generalized framework?
9. Which sectors matter most (finance, healthcare, public sector, education, e-commerce)?
10. Which threat actors are most relevant (external attacker, insider, competitor, nation-state, script-kiddie)?

## 3) Red-team depth
11. Which attack classes are mandatory to include?
12. Do you want proof-of-concept prompts in the appendix?
13. Should we include model extraction/inversion or keep to application-layer attacks?
14. Should social engineering of human operators be covered?
15. How technical should attack walkthroughs be (high, medium, low)?

## 4) Blue-team depth
16. Do you want controls mapped by SDLC phase, architecture layer, or both?
17. Which controls are non-negotiable in your argument?
18. Do you prefer prevention-heavy or detection-and-response-heavy strategy?
19. Should we include governance/compliance controls or stay purely technical?
20. Which KPIs do you want to report as success metrics?

## 5) Unified solution design
21. What do you want to call your unified framework/model?
22. Should it be prescriptive (strict steps) or adaptable (modular playbook)?
23. Do you want maturity levels (Level 1-4) in the framework?
24. Should it include a cost/effort dimension for organizations?
25. Do you want a formal algorithm/workflow diagram in the final paper?

## 6) Evidence and literature
26. How many core references do you want in the first complete draft?
27. Do you already have mandatory sources (papers, OWASP, NIST, ISO docs)?
28. Do you need citation style constraints from DHBW (or your supervisor)?
29. Should we prioritize peer-reviewed sources over industry reports?
30. Which years should be prioritized (e.g., 2023-2026 for LLM security)?

## 7) Writing style preferences
31. Should writing tone be strongly academic, mixed academic-practical, or practitioner-oriented?
32. Should sections open with definitions, examples, or argument statements?
33. How long should each main section be (approximate page targets)?
34. Do you prefer short dense paragraphs or longer narrative paragraphs?
35. How strict should we be with repeated structure per section?

## 8) Language and formatting
36. Should final text be fully English, fully German, or mixed?
37. Should glossary definitions be concise (1 line) or explanatory (2-4 lines)?
38. Do you want acronyms expanded at first use in every chapter or only once globally?
39. Should we include tables for threat-to-control mapping?
40. Should every section end with a short "Key Takeaways" block?

## 9) Collaboration workflow
41. When I draft sections, do you want full section drafts or incremental paragraph batches?
42. Do you want me to always propose alternatives before finalizing a section?
43. Do you want automatic consistency checks (terms, acronyms, thesis alignment) every turn?
44. Do you want me to maintain a live "open issues" list while writing?
45. How should I flag uncertain claims (inline note, footnote, TODO list)?

## 10) Skill behavior (future Codex skill)
46. Should the skill force a fixed section template every time?
47. Should the skill include a mandatory quality gate checklist before each deliverable?
48. Should the skill auto-generate reviewer questions per section?
49. Should the skill maintain a reusable library of your preferred phrases and transitions?
50. What should the skill optimize for first: speed, depth, citation quality, or publication readiness?
